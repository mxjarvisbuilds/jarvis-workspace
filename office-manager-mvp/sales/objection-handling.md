# Objection Handling

## “I already have a system / CRM”
That’s fine — I’m not trying to replace what already works. This fills the gaps most CRMs still leave open: missed calls, stale estimates, owner/office/tech handoff, and review protection. If your current setup is already catching all that cleanly, great. Most aren’t.

## “My office manager handles this”
Good — this should make your office manager stronger, not replace them. The point is to handle repetitive follow-up fast and consistently so nothing falls through when the day gets busy, while urgent jobs still go to a human. Human judgment still matters most on urgent and high-value jobs.

## “How much does it cost?”
It’s $199 to set up, the first month is free, and then it’s $497 a month after that. The real question is whether it recovers more revenue than it costs, and that’s exactly why the offer has both a free proof window and a guarantee.

## “What if the AI screws up with a customer?”
That’s why the scope is tight. It handles first response, basic intake, and follow-up — and anything urgent or high-value gets kicked to a human immediately. It is not trying to coach techs, record every call for surveillance, or freelance with your reputation. The system is built to stay in its lane instead of freelancing with your reputation.

## “I don’t want to lose the personal touch”
You shouldn’t. This doesn’t replace the personal touch — it buys you more chances to actually use it. Fast response plus human takeover where it counts feels more personal than a missed call and no follow-up.

## “I’ve been burned by automation/marketing people before”
Yeah, a lot of contractors have. That’s exactly why this is tied to a simple outcome: recovered revenue, not pretty dashboards and vague promises. If it doesn’t make more than it costs, the guarantee is there for a reason.

## “I need to think about it”
Totally fair. Usually the real question is whether you believe missed calls and dead estimates are costing you enough to matter. If they are, waiting usually just means bleeding a little longer.

## “Can you do a free trial?”
You’re already getting the first month free. That gives you a real chance to see it work without me giving away a custom build with no commitment on your side.

## “What if I cancel?”
If you invoke the guarantee inside 60 days, you can exit there. If not, you ride out the 3-month minimum after the free month, then it goes month-to-month with 30 days’ notice. That’s fair on both sides.

## “Will this annoy my customers?”
Not if it’s done right, and that’s the whole point of the build. One fast response, one clean intake, one booking nudge, controlled follow-up windows, and review gating — it’s meant to feel helpful, not spammy.

## “I’m too busy to set up another thing”
That’s exactly the kind of business this is for. The setup exists so you stop depending on memory and spare time to follow up on money already sitting in your pipeline.

## “Why the 3-month minimum?”
Because I’m giving you a free month to prove it works. All I’m asking is that if it does work, you give it a fair shot to compound instead of judging it before the follow-up and review cycles have time to do their job.

## “I don’t want AI watching my techs”
It doesn’t. This is not surveillance AI or a coaching tool. The first job is recovering missed calls and stale estimates. The field-team benefit is cleaner handoff information, not watching or grading technicians.

## “Will this mess with dispatch?”
Not at first. The opening wedge is lead recovery. The next layer is lightweight dispatch/customer-status support only after the same job record is working cleanly. It should plug into how you already work, not force a full FSM replacement.
